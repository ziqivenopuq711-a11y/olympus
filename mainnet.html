Mainnet Assets

Live production contracts and applications deployed on Ethereum mainnet that are eligible for the Olympus Bug Bounty program. All testing must be done on local forks — never on live mainnet.

The Olympus protocol operates primarily on Ethereum mainnet, where the core treasury, staking, and bonding mechanisms are deployed. All live OHM token operations, treasury management, and governance execution happen on mainnet. These contracts hold substantial protocol-owned liquidity and user funds, making them the highest-priority targets in the bug bounty program.

Researchers are encouraged to fork mainnet using tools such as Hardhat, Foundry, or Anvil to reproduce vulnerabilities in a local environment before submitting. Direct interaction with live mainnet contracts is strictly prohibited under the program rules.

Accepted Impacts — Mainnet

Critical & High Severity Impacts
Direct theft of user OHM or sOHM funds
Any vulnerability enabling an attacker to steal staked or unstaked OHM from user wallets or protocol-managed accounts.
Unauthorized extraction of treasury assets
Bugs that allow assets to be removed from the treasury without proper authorization or governance approval.
Unauthorized OHM minting
Vulnerabilities that allow minting OHM without the correct authorization, diluting the supply and backing ratio.
Bond pricing manipulation
Exploits that allow acquiring bonds at an improperly discounted rate, causing a loss of treasury value.
Governance manipulation or bypass
Attacks that allow bypassing governance votes or executing unauthorized governance actions.

Out of Scope — Mainnet

The following are explicitly excluded from the mainnet scope and will not be eligible for rewards:

  • Rebalancing or accounting errors where assets remain within treasury or protocol-controlled contracts
  • Manipulations that do not result in net value extraction from the system
  • Vulnerabilities in third-party integrations not maintained by OlympusDAO
  • Issues in already-disclosed audit findings that have not been patched
  • Economic attacks relying solely on market conditions without a smart contract vulnerability
  • Gas optimization suggestions or theoretical improvements without security impact
  • Informational findings with no practical exploit path

When in doubt, researchers are encouraged to submit anyway — the Bug Bounty Management team will assess eligibility on a case-by-case basis for borderline submissions.
