Program Overview

Olympus is building the value layer of global finance — a programmatic, treasury-backed framework for monetary policy, credit, and liquidity. The protocol is governed by OlympusDAO and powers the OHM token, a decentralized reserve currency designed to maintain long-term purchasing power through an ever-growing protocol-owned treasury.

This bug bounty program covers Olympus smart contracts deployed on Ethereum mainnet and Polygon, as well as the official web application. The program is managed by OlympusDAO under the provisions of OIP-38 and is focused on preventing loss of treasury funds, user funds, and bond funds.

Rewards are paid in OHM and denominated in USD. Critical vulnerabilities may qualify for up to $3,333,333. The program operates under the Primacy of Rules — all rewards are governed strictly by the terms on this page.

Olympus adheres to Responsible Publication Category 3 — Approval Required. Researchers must obtain written approval from the OlympusDAO Bug Bounty Management team before publicly disclosing any submitted finding.

Impacts in Scope

The following categories of impacts qualify for rewards under this program. Only direct financial impacts are considered; theoretical or indirect impacts are generally out of scope.

  • Unauthorized extraction of assets from the treasury
  • Direct theft or loss of user OHM or sOHM funds
  • Permanent freezing of treasury-held assets
  • Manipulation of the bonding mechanism leading to improperly discounted purchases
  • Smart contract bugs enabling reentrancy or flash-loan-based exploits
  • Unauthorized minting of OHM tokens
  • Front-end exploits directly causing financial loss (e.g., malicious transaction injection)

For web/app vulnerabilities, only those directly leading to financial harm listed above are accepted. All other web vulnerabilities (XSS without direct financial impact, UI bugs, etc.) are out of scope.

Program Rules

Eligibility & Submission Requirements
Proof of Concept Required
A working PoC demonstrating the bug's impact is required for all severity levels. Submissions without a PoC will not be considered.
No Prior Disclosure
Bugs that have already been publicly disclosed or submitted in a prior report are ineligible for reward.
Not Available to Olympus Contributors
Olympus contributors must submit via the official Olympus Discord channel and should contact the Bug Bounty Management team directly.
KYC Not Required
No KYC information is required for payout processing under this program.
Prohibited Activities
Testing on mainnet or public testnets — all testing must be done on local forks
Any testing with pricing oracles or third-party smart contracts
Phishing, social engineering, or denial-of-service attacks against project assets
Automated testing generating significant traffic or public disclosure of unpatched vulnerabilities
Primacy of Rules

Olympus adheres to the Primacy of Rules, meaning the entire bug bounty program is operated strictly under the terms stated on this page. Rewards are calculated based on documented rules, not discretionary judgment, except where the program explicitly grants discretion (e.g., Tier 3 awards).

The reward calculation for critical vulnerabilities is based on 10% of the directly affected funds, up to a maximum of $3,333,333. The OHM amount is determined using the average price between CoinMarketCap.com and CoinGecko.com at the time of submission. No adjustments are made for liquidity constraints.
